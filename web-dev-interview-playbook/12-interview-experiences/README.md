# Interview Experience Contributions

Real interview reports from students — company names, round types, questions asked, and honest outcomes.

## Why This Section Exists

- Syllabus prep doesn't tell you **what Infosys Round 2 actually feels like**
- Knowing others got stumped on `useEffect` deps helps you prioritize
- "Selected" and "rejected" experiences both teach something

## How to Contribute

1. Copy [experience-template.md](./experience-template.md)
2. Create a new file: `experiences/company-role-year.md` (folder optional)
3. Fill in every section honestly — redact personal info if needed
4. Open a PR or share with your placement cell

**Anonymize if needed:** "Mid-size Bangalore startup" is fine instead of exact company name.

## What Makes a Good Experience Write-up

| Good | Not helpful |
|------|-------------|
| Exact question phrasing | "They asked JS questions" |
| What follow-ups came after | "It was hard" |
| What you wish you'd prepared | Only outcome bragging |
| Difficulty rating with reason | Copy-paste question dumps with no context |
| Honest result + learnings | Fake or exaggerated stories |

## How to Use Others' Experiences

1. **Search by round type** — filtering React-heavy startups vs DSA-heavy service companies
2. **Collect recurring questions** — if 5 people got `event loop`, study it
3. **Calibrate difficulty** — "Medium" at one company may match your "Hard"
4. **Don't memorize company-specific answers** — patterns transfer; exact questions rarely repeat

## Privacy & Ethics

- Don't share proprietary coding questions from ongoing hiring drives
- Don't name interviewers
- Don't share internal company tools or credentials
- Do share question **patterns** and your preparation advice

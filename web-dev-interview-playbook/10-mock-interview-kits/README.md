## How to Run a Mock Interview

### Setup (5 minutes before)

1. **Pick a role:** Interviewer reads the script; candidate does not peek ahead.
2. **Set a timer:** Use a phone timer for each section. Real interviews are timed.
3. **Choose environment:** Video call (realistic) or in-person with a whiteboard/paper.
4. **Candidate brings:** Resume, one project ready to discuss, IDE or paper for live tasks.

### During the Interview

- Candidate answers **out loud** — no reading from notes.
- Interviewer asks **at least 2 follow-ups** per main question.
- If candidate is stuck for 30+ seconds, interviewer gives a small hint (note that you needed a hint).
- Live tasks: candidate explains approach **before** writing code.

### After the Interview (Debrief — 10 min)

1. Score using [evaluation-rubric.md](./evaluation-rubric.md).
2. Candidate asks: "Which answers were weak? Did I communicate tradeoffs?"
3. Assign homework: redo weak topics from the relevant section in the playbook.
4. **Schedule a retry** in 3–5 days.

## Tips for Interviewers (Friends/Mentors)

- Don't be too nice. Ask follow-ups even when the answer sounds right.
- Say "Can you go deeper?" or "What would break if…?" often.
- For live tasks, watch **process** more than perfect syntax.
- Use the rubric honestly. A score of 2/5 on communication is useful feedback.

## Tips for Candidates

- Treat every mock like the real thing: dress decently, camera on, no phone.
- Record yourself at least once — you'll notice filler words and rambling.
- Connect answers to **your project** whenever possible.
- It's okay to say "I don't know, but here's how I'd figure it out."

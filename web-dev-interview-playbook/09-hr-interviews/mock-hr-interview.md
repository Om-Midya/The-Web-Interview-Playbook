# HR Interview: Mock Practice (30 Minutes)

Use this script with a friend. One person is interviewer, one is candidate. Switch roles after.

**Rules:**
- Candidate answers out loud, no reading from notes
- Answers should be 60–90 seconds (interviewer times them)
- Interviewer asks at least 1 follow-up per question

---

## Section 1: Opening (5 min)

1. "Tell me about yourself."
   - Follow-up: "You mentioned [project] — what was your specific role?"
2. "Why are you interested in this role?"
   - Follow-up: "What do you know about our company?"
3. "Walk me through your resume briefly."
   - Follow-up: "What's the project you're most proud of?"

*Interviewer: Note if candidate stays under 90 seconds and mentions the company.*

---

## Section 2: Behavioral Questions (12 min)

Pick 4 questions. Candidate uses STAR format.

1. "Tell me about a time you worked in a team."
   - Follow-up: "What would you do differently?"
2. "Describe a time you faced a tight deadline."
   - Follow-up: "How did you communicate with your team about the delay risk?"
3. "Tell me about a time you failed or made a mistake."
   - Follow-up: "What did you learn?"
4. "Give an example of when you showed leadership."
   - Follow-up: "Were you officially the leader, or did you step up?"

**Alternative questions (swap if needed):**
- "Tell me about a conflict with a teammate."
- "Describe a time you had to learn something quickly."
- "Tell me about a time you took initiative."

*Interviewer: STAR format? Specific details? Takes ownership?*

---

## Section 3: Self-Awareness (5 min)

1. "What are your greatest strengths?"
   - Follow-up: "Give me a specific example of [strength they mentioned]."
2. "What is your greatest weakness?"
   - Follow-up: "What are you doing to improve it?"
3. "Where do you see yourself in 3 years?"

*Interviewer: Strengths have evidence? Weakness is real but not fatal? Career goals are realistic?*

---

## Section 4: Situational & Fit (5 min)

1. "Why are you leaving your current situation?" (or "Why not pursue higher studies?")
2. "How do you handle stress or multiple deadlines?"
3. "Are you open to [relocation / hybrid / night shifts]?" *(interviewer picks relevant one)*

---

## Section 5: Candidate Questions (3 min)

**Interviewer:** "Do you have any questions for me?"

*Candidate should ask 2–3 prepared questions. Examples:*

- "What does a typical day look like for someone in this role?"
- "How does the team handle mentorship for junior developers?"
- "What are the biggest challenges the team is facing right now?"
- "What does success look like in the first 90 days?"
- "What's the team culture like?"

**Bad questions to avoid:**
- "What does your company do?" (should already know)
- "How much vacation do I get?" (save for offer stage)
- "Can I work fully remote?" (unless they advertised it)

---

## Evaluation Rubric

| Area | 1 (Weak) | 3 (Good) | 5 (Strong) |
|------|----------|----------|------------|
| Tell me about yourself | Rambling, unfocused | Clear 90-sec pitch | Tailored, ends with why this company |
| STAR stories | Vague, no structure | STAR format, some detail | Specific, quantified, shows learning |
| Self-awareness | Cliché answers | Honest with examples | Strengths evidenced, weakness + growth plan |
| Communication | One-word answers | Clear, audible | Confident, concise, engaging |
| Questions for them | "No questions" | 1 generic question | 2–3 thoughtful, researched questions |
| Professionalism | Late, casual, negative | On time, polite | Positive framing, enthusiastic |

---

## Red Flags (Interviewer Notes)

- [ ] Speaks negatively about professors, teammates, or previous employers
- [ ] Can't describe any project in detail
- [ ] Says "I have no weaknesses"
- [ ] No questions for the interviewer
- [ ] Doesn't know what the company does
- [ ] Answers exceed 2 minutes (rambling)

## Green Flags

- [ ] Quantifies results ("200 users," "reduced load time by 40%")
- [ ] Takes ownership of failures
- [ ] Shows genuine enthusiasm for the role
- [ ] Researched the company
- [ ] Asks about team culture and growth

---

## Debrief (3 min)

**Candidate asks:**
- Did my "tell me about yourself" feel natural or rehearsed?
- Were my STAR stories specific enough?
- Did I talk too much or too little?

**Interviewer gives:**
- Score /30 (6 areas × 5 points)
- One thing to keep
- One thing to improve before the real interview

---

## Day-Before Checklist

- [ ] "Tell me about yourself" rehearsed (< 90 seconds)
- [ ] 5 STAR stories prepared
- [ ] Strength + weakness with examples ready
- [ ] 3 questions to ask the interviewer
- [ ] Company researched (product, news, values)
- [ ] Job description read — keywords noted
- [ ] Professional outfit ready
- [ ] Video call tested (if virtual)

---

## Quick Reference: STAR Timing

| Part | Time | Content |
|------|------|---------|
| Situation | 10 sec | Context |
| Task | 10 sec | Your responsibility |
| Action | 40 sec | What YOU did (bulk of answer) |
| Result | 15 sec | Outcome + learning |
| **Total** | **~75 sec** | |

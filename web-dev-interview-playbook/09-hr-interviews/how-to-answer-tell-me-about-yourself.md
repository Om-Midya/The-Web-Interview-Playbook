# How to Answer "Tell Me About Yourself"

The most common opening question in every HR and technical interview. A strong answer sets the tone for the entire conversation.

---

## The Rules

1. **60–90 seconds** — not your life story
2. **Past → Present → Future** structure
3. **Relevant to THIS job** — not everything you've ever done
4. **End with why you're here** — why this company, this role
5. **Practice out loud** — it should feel natural, not memorized

---

## The Formula

```
[Present]  Who you are now (student, focus area)
[Past]     1–2 relevant highlights (project, internship, achievement)
[Future]   Why you're excited about THIS role at THIS company
```

---

## Template 1: Final-Year CS Student (No Internship)

> "I'm **[Name]**, a final-year **Computer Science** student at **[University]** with a focus on **web development**.
>
> Over the past two years, I've built several fullstack projects — most recently **[Project Name]**, a **[one-line description]** using **React, Node.js, and PostgreSQL**. I handled everything from database design to deployment on Railway.
>
> Before that, I was active in our college **coding club** where I organized weekly web dev workshops for 30+ juniors. That experience taught me how to explain technical concepts clearly — which I enjoy.
>
> I'm now looking for a **frontend developer internship** where I can contribute to real products while learning from experienced engineers. **[Company]** excites me because **[specific reason — their product, tech blog, open source, mission]**."

**Customize the bracketed parts for every interview.**

---

## Template 2: Final-Year IT Student (With Internship)

> "I'm **[Name]**, completing my **B.Tech in Information Technology** from **[University]**, graduating in **[Month Year]**.
>
> I recently completed a **3-month internship at [Company]** where I worked on **[specific work — e.g., migrating legacy jQuery components to React]**. I shipped **[specific deliverable]** that **[result — e.g., improved page load time by 40%]**.
>
> In college, I built **[Project Name]** — a **[description]** that **[result — e.g., is used by 50+ students on campus]**. I'm comfortable across the stack but especially enjoy **frontend development and UI/UX**.
>
> I'm applying for the **[Role]** at **[Company]** because **[specific reason]**. I'd love to bring my React experience and eagerness to learn to your team."

---

## Template 3: Career Switcher / Bootcamp Graduate

> "I'm **[Name]**. I spent **[X years]** working as a **[previous role]** before transitioning into software development.
>
> I completed **[bootcamp/self-study program]** where I built **[2–3 projects briefly]**. My strongest project is **[Project Name]** — **[one-line description with tech stack and outcome]**.
>
> My previous career taught me **[transferable skill — communication, project management, domain knowledge]** which I bring to engineering work. For example, **[brief example]**.
>
> I'm seeking a **junior developer role** at **[Company]** because **[specific reason]**. I'm committed to growing as an engineer and your **[team/product/mission]** aligns with where I want to build my career."

---

## Template 4: M.Tech / Postgraduate Student

> "I'm **[Name]**, an **M.Tech student in Computer Science** at **[University]**, specializing in **[area if any]**.
>
> My thesis work involves **[brief description]**, which strengthened my skills in **[relevant skills — algorithms, data analysis, systems]**.
>
> Alongside academics, I built **[Project Name]** — a production-deployed **[description]**. I also contributed to **[open source / research project / teaching assistant role]**.
>
> I'm looking for a **fullstack / backend role** at **[Company]** to apply my strong fundamentals in a product-focused environment. I'm particularly drawn to **[specific company detail]**."

---

## What to Include vs Skip

| Include ✅ | Skip ❌ |
|-----------|---------|
| Current status (student, graduating when) | Childhood, school years |
| Tech focus aligned to the job | Every course you've taken |
| 1–2 strongest projects or experiences | Full resume chronology |
| One quantified result if possible | Hobbies (unless directly relevant) |
| Why THIS company | Generic "I want to learn" without specifics |

---

## Customization Checklist (Do This for Every Interview)

Before each interview, edit your answer:

- [ ] Replace company name and specific reason you're interested
- [ ] Match job title (frontend, backend, fullstack, intern)
- [ ] Highlight the project/skill most relevant to the JD
- [ ] Check the job description for keywords to mirror
- [ ] Research one recent company news item to reference if natural

**Example customization:**

Job posting says: *"React, TypeScript, collaborative team environment"*

Your answer should mention: *"React projects"*, *"team project where I collaborated"*, and ideally *"TypeScript"* if true.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| 5-minute autobiography | Cut to 90 seconds max |
| "I'm a fresher with no experience" | Reframe: projects ARE experience |
| No mention of the company | Always end with why them |
| Monotone recitation | Practice until conversational |
| Only academics, no projects | Lead with what you've built |
| Lying or exaggerating | Interviewers will probe deeper |

---

## Practice Exercise

1. Pick the template closest to your situation
2. Fill in all bracketed parts
3. Read aloud — time yourself
4. Cut anything over 90 seconds
5. Record on phone — watch back
6. Repeat until it sounds like you're telling a friend, not reading a script

---

## Follow-Up Questions to Expect

After "Tell me about yourself," interviewers often ask:

- "Tell me more about [project you mentioned]."
- "What was your role in that team project?"
- "Why are you interested in web development specifically?"
- "Why are you leaving your current path?" (career switchers)
- "What do you know about our company?"

Have 2-sentence answers ready for each.

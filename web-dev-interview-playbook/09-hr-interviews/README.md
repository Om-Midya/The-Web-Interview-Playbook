# HR Interview Preparation

HR rounds test **fit, communication, and professionalism** — not coding. Many students lose offers here despite strong technical skills. Don't be one of them.

## What HR Interviewers Evaluate

- Can you communicate clearly?
- Will you be a good teammate?
- Are you genuinely interested in this company/role?
- Do you have self-awareness and growth mindset?
- Will you show up, learn, and stay?

## The STAR Method (Use for Every Behavioral Question)

| Letter | Meaning | Tip |
|--------|---------|-----|
| **S** | Situation | Set the scene in 1–2 sentences |
| **T** | Task | What was YOUR responsibility? |
| **A** | Action | What did YOU do? (most of the answer) |
| **R** | Result | Quantify if possible; what did you learn? |

**Timing:** 60–90 seconds per answer. Not 3 minutes.

## Before Every HR Interview

- [ ] Research the company (website, recent news, products)
- [ ] Read the job description — align answers to it
- [ ] Prepare 5 STAR stories (see behavioral-questions.md themes)
- [ ] Prepare 2–3 questions to ask them
- [ ] Professional attire (even for video calls)
- [ ] Test camera/mic if virtual

## Time Estimate

- **Write your stories:** 2–3 hours (one sitting)
- **Practice mock:** 30 minutes with a friend
- **Per interview prep:** 30 minutes researching the company
